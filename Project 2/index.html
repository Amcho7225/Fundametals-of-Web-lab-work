About Us

Vault-Tec Industries, a prominent pre-war corporation, specialized in constructing underground Vaults, vital shelters designed to protect against nuclear fallout. Founded in the mid-21st century, Vault-Tec played a pivotal role in government preparations for nuclear war. Renowned for its innovative technology and visionary engineering, Vault-Tec's mission extended beyond construction to encompass comprehensive community development and resource management. With a commitment to safeguarding humanity's future, Vault-Tec stood as a beacon of hope amidst global uncertainty, dedicated to preserving life and nurturing resilient societies in the face of impending catastrophe.

Stanley Van Robb

Founder and CEO

Stanley Van Robb was one of the founding members of Vault-Tec Industries. As the CEO, he played a pivotal role in shaping the company's vision and overseeing its operations. With a keen business acumen and a relentless drive for success, Van Robb was instrumental in Vault-Tec's rise to prominence as a leading provider of vault technology.

Robert House

Strategic Advisor

Robert House was a brilliant entrepreneur and technologist who collaborated closely with Vault-Tec in the development of vault technology. While not directly involved in day-to-day operations, House provided strategic guidance and expertise to Vault-Tec's leadership, leveraging his vast resources and influence to advance the company's goals.

T. Stanislaus Braun

Chief Scientist

T. Stanislaus Braun was the enigmatic genius behind many of Vault-Tec's scientific breakthroughs. As the Chief Scientist, he led the company's research and development efforts, pioneering innovative solutions to the challenges of post-nuclear survival. Braun's unconventional methods and eccentric personality made him a controversial figure within Vault-Tec, but his contributions to the company's success were undeniable.
